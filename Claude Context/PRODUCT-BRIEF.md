**Last Updated:** January 2026

## Vision

**ThoughtFolio transforms passive knowledge consumption into active application.**

Most people capture insights but never revisit them. Highlights sit unread. Notes gather dust. The gap between learning and doing remains wide.

ThoughtFolio closes this gap by becoming your knowledge accountability partner â€” capturing your best insights and proactively surfacing them at moments they can actually change your behavior.

## Positioning

### What ThoughtFolio Is

- A knowledge accountability partner
- A system for applying what you learn
- A curator of your most important insights organized by life context
- A coach that knows your values and reminds you of them

### What ThoughtFolio Is Not

- A note-taking app (we're not competing with Notion or Obsidian)
- A read-later service (we're not Pocket or Instapaper)
- A highlight aggregator (we're not Readwise, though we serve similar users)
- An AI that generates thoughts for you (your knowledge, your words)

### Competitive Differentiation

| Tool | What They Do | What's Missing |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Readwise** | Aggregates highlights, resurfaces randomly | No contextual timing, no behavior change focus |
| **Notion** | Stores everything | No proactive surfacing, becomes a graveyard |
| **Google NotebookLM** | AI-powered research | No personal knowledge, no accountability |
| **Journaling Apps** | Reflection capture | No resurfacing, no application prompts |

**ThoughtFolio's unique combination:**
1. Context-based curation with accountability (Active List of 10)
2. Proactive, context-aware surfacing
3. Accountability mechanisms (daily prompts, moments)
4. Your knowledge, not AI-generated content
5. URL-based extraction from articles and videos
6. AI-powered discovery of new relevant content

## Core Principles

### 1. Focused Accountability

The Active List (10 thoughts max) isn't a restriction â€” it's a feature. While users can capture unlimited thoughts across contexts, the Active List forces them to choose what matters most right now. These are the thoughts that will appear in daily prompts.

**Implication:** We encourage broad capture but focused attention. The Active List represents "what I'm working on applying."

### 2. Application Over Accumulation

Success isn't measured in thoughts captured. It's measured in knowledge applied. Every feature should ask: "Does this help users actually use their insights?"

**Implication:** Daily prompts include action suggestions. Moments focus on preparation. We track "applied" status, not just "captured."

### 3. Right Thought, Right Time

Random resurfacing isn't enough. The right knowledge at the wrong time is noise. Context matters.

**Implication:** Calendar integration, individual scheduling, and moment matching all serve this principle. We invest in timing intelligence.

### 4. Organized by Life

Different life areas need different knowledge. Coding insights don't help in parenting moments. Relationship advice doesn't apply to technical problems.

**Implication:** Contexts let users organize by life domain. Moments search all contexts but surface contextually relevant thoughts.

### 5. User's Words, User's Knowledge

We help users capture and resurface their own insights. We don't generate knowledge for them. The AI assists extraction, discovery, and matching, but the substance comes from the user.

**Implication:** AI suggestions are always editable. We never put words in users' mouths. The thoughts belong to the user. Even in Discover, users edit before saving.

### 6. Gentle Accountability

We're a supportive coach, not a nagging reminder app. Accountability should feel empowering, not guilt-inducing.

**Implication:** Missed prompts don't stack up. No streaks or punitive mechanics. Encouragement over obligation.

## Target User

### Primary: The Intentional Learner

People who:
- Read books, listen to podcasts, consume content deliberately
- Care about personal growth and self-improvement
- Have tried (and been frustrated by) other note-taking systems
- Want their learning to actually change their behavior
- Have multiple life domains they want to improve (work, health, relationships)

### Not Our User (Initially)

- Casual content consumers who don't reflect on what they read
- Users who want AI to generate insights for them
- People looking for a general-purpose note app
- Teams or organizations (we're B2C focused initially)

## Success Vision

### 6-Month Success

- Users report "I actually applied something I captured" moments
- Thought collections feel organized by life context, not cluttered
- Daily prompts feel relevant, not random
- Users recommend to fellow learners
- Average user has 3+ contexts with thoughts in each
- Discovery feature drives 20%+ of new thought creation

### 12-Month Success

- ThoughtFolio is "where my best insights live"
- Users have stories of knowledge applied at key moments
- Word-of-mouth drives growth among intentional learners
- Clear retention signal: users return weekly
- URL extraction and Discovery are primary capture methods
